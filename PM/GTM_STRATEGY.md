# Blood Insight — Go-To-Market Strategy (Lab Focus)

> **Note on pricing:** This document references $200–$500/mo as example figures. The agreed pricing tiers are in `BUSINESS_OUTLINE.md` — Starter at $599/mo, Growth at $1,400/mo, Scale at $2,800/mo. Use those numbers in actual outreach.

---

This is a classic B2B SaaS play. The target isn't the giants like LabCorp or Quest — they have custom-built (though often clunky) portals. The goldmine is the **Independent Clinical Laboratory**: small-to-mid-sized labs that struggle to compete with the big guys on tech but win on local service.

---

## 1. How to Find "The Small Guys"

Target labs with enough volume to care about retention but not so large they have an in-house dev team.

### The CMS QCOR Database (US Cheat Code)

Every US lab must be CLIA-certified. Use the official **[CMS QCOR (Quality Certification and Reporting)](https://qcor.cms.gov/main.jsp)** tool to find them.

- **Filter for:** Certificate of Compliance or Certificate of Accreditation
- **Avoid:** "Certificate of Waiver" labs — these are usually tiny doctor offices doing basic tests
- **Target:** "Independent" or "Physician Office Labs (POL)" doing Moderate to High Complexity testing
- **Start local:** Local labs are far more likely to take a meeting with a "local tech partner"

### Google Maps + "Pathology"

Search for **"Independent Pathology Lab"** or **"Clinical Laboratory"** in mid-sized cities.

- **Green flag:** Website looks like it was built in 2012 and they have no modern patient login portal — that's your #1 target.

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Search for the following titles at companies with 11–50 employees:

| Title | What they care about |
|---|---|
| Lab Director | Compliance and accuracy |
| Lab Manager | Workflow and "PDF hell" |
| Owner / CEO | Money and patient retention |

## 2. The Outreach Strategy

Don't lead with "I have a dashboard." Lead with **Patient Lifetime Value (LTV)**.

Smaller labs lose a massive amount of revenue because patients get one test, receive a confusing PDF via email, and never come back. The product solves **revenue leakage** — not just data visualization.

### Cold Email Template

> **Subject:** Helping [Lab Name] patients actually understand their results (and come back)
>
> Hi [Name], I noticed [Lab Name] provides [Specific Test] services. Most labs send PDFs that patients can't read, leading to a "one-and-done" relationship.
>
> I built a white-label dashboard that turns those PDFs into a branded experience for your patients — complete with automated reminders for their next test. Can I send you a 2-minute video of how it looks with your logo on it?

### Physical "Before vs. After" Mailer (High Success Rate)

Print a side-by-side comparison:
- **Left:** A messy, standard lab PDF
- **Right:** A beautiful, branded screenshot of the dashboard with their logo

Mail it to the Lab Director. A physical visual of their brand looking better is hard to ignore.

## 3. Deal Structures

Since pricing is flexible, use that to lower the barrier to entry.

| Model | Best For | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-Report Fee** | Small, growing labs | They only pay when they use it (e.g. $0.50/PDF processed) — low risk for them |
| **Monthly SaaS Fee** | Established local labs | Predictable for both sides — see `BUSINESS_OUTLINE.md` for agreed tiers |
| **License / Buyout** | One-off white-label deals | Flat fee (e.g. $10k) + small annual maintenance — good for labs that want to "own" it |

---

## 4. Killer Features to Pitch

To a small lab owner, the dashboard is "neat" but these features **close the deal**:

1. **White-Labeling** — "It's not my product; it's *your* lab's portal." Builds their brand equity.
2. **Next-Test Reminders** — "If a patient has high cholesterol, the system pings them in 6 months to re-test." Directly increases lab revenue.
3. **Mobile Friendly** — Most lab PDFs are unreadable on a smartphone. This isn't.
4. **HIPAA Compliance** — Non-negotiable permission to play. Be ready to explain exactly how data is encrypted and stored.

### The "Test Drive" Close

Offer to take one of their existing sample PDFs and re-skin it in the dashboard for free. Once they see their own data looking like a polished Silicon Valley app, the emotional buy happens before the sales conversation even starts.

## 5. Key Open Question

What's the current state of the PDF-to-data extraction — LLM-based or rigid OCR templates? This matters for how fast the product can scale to different lab report formats. LLM-based is more flexible but needs guardrails; template-based is more reliable but requires per-lab setup time.
